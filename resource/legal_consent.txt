This document is the informed consent you give KnowYourGenes
("KYG", operated by BFG Market Consult Private Limited (operating the KnowYourGenes brand)) before we collect and analyse
your DNA. You must read and accept this consent before the
phlebotomist draws your sample. A signed digital copy is retained
in your account.

If you are booking for a minor or a dependent adult unable to
consent themselves, the parent, legal guardian, or attorney-in-fact
provides this consent on their behalf.


1. WHAT YOU ARE CONSENTING TO
-----------------------------
By signing this consent you confirm that:

  (a) You are providing a biological sample (saliva and/or blood)
      voluntarily and free of coercion.
  (b) You authorise KnowYourGenes and its partner NABL-accredited
      laboratory to extract DNA from this sample, sequence it,
      and analyse it against the panel(s) of genes corresponding
      to the package you have purchased.
  (c) You authorise KnowYourGenes to generate a written report
      from this analysis and deliver it to you over the email,
      WhatsApp, and account login you have provided.
  (d) You authorise the de-identified storage of your sample at
      the partner lab for up to 60 days, and the storage of your
      raw genetic data ("VCF") on KYG-controlled servers in India
      for as long as you maintain your account, unless you ask
      for it to be deleted.


2. WHAT YOU UNDERSTAND
----------------------
You confirm that you have read and understood the following:

  - The genetic test is for informational and investigational
    purposes only. It is not a medical diagnosis.
  - The report should be interpreted by a qualified healthcare
    professional. KnowYourGenes does not practise medicine.
  - Genetic findings are probabilistic. They indicate
    predisposition, not certainty. The absence of a risk variant
    does not mean you are protected from the relevant condition.
  - A report may reveal information that is unexpected,
    distressing, or that has implications for biological
    relatives (especially in cancer-risk and reproductive
    panels). You acknowledge this possibility and choose to
    proceed.
  - Genetic information may have implications for life insurance
    or other matters in jurisdictions outside India. You have
    considered this before consenting.
  - You have the option of pre-test and post-test counselling
    with a qualified genetic counsellor. For cancer-risk and
    reproductive panels, KYG strongly recommends both.


3. WHAT KNOWYOURGENES WILL NOT DO
---------------------------------
KnowYourGenes will not:

  - Perform pre-natal sex determination of a foetus. This is
    prohibited under the PCPNDT Act, 1994 and KYG will not
    process any request that breaches this law.
  - Sell or share your genetic data with insurers, lenders, or
    advertisers.
  - Use your genetic data to train any third-party AI / ML
    model.
  - Use your genetic data for research without your separate,
    opt-in consent (see clause 5).
  - Disclose your report to any third party (including a
    spouse, parent of an adult, or employer) without your
    explicit written authorisation, except where compelled by
    a court order or applicable law.


4. RISKS AND LIMITATIONS
------------------------
You acknowledge that:

  - A small fraction of samples (around 1 in 200) fail at the
    extraction or sequencing step and require re-collection.
  - The genes analysed are limited to the panel of your
    purchased package. The test does not screen for all known
    genetic conditions.
  - Variants of uncertain significance ("VUS") may be reported.
    A VUS means the evidence is currently inconclusive and may
    be reclassified in the future as more research accumulates.
  - Variants associated with serious adult-onset conditions
    (some cancer-risk and cardiac variants) carry implications
    for biological relatives. KYG cannot contact your relatives
    on your behalf.
  - The report is based on the scientific evidence current at
    the time of issue. Future re-analysis is available on
    request, subject to fees in force at that time.


5. OPTIONAL - RESEARCH PARTICIPATION (SEPARATE OPT-IN)
------------------------------------------------------
You may, separately and entirely at your option, consent to your
de-identified genetic data and self-reported phenotype information
being included in research datasets that KYG or its academic
partners may use to improve genetic interpretation for Indian
populations.

Research consent is independent of the test purchase. You can
withdraw research consent at any time from your account, and
your data will be removed from any future dataset; data already
included in a closed dataset cannot retroactively be removed but
will remain de-identified.


6. WITHDRAWAL OF CONSENT
------------------------
You may withdraw consent for the storage of your genetic data, or
for any specific use of it, at any time, by writing to
privacy@kyg.in or from "Privacy" in your account settings.

  - Withdrawal of storage consent: your raw genetic data and
    report are deleted within 60 days. Statutory minimums
    (invoice records, audit logs) are retained.
  - Withdrawal of consent during processing: if your sample is
    already at the lab, processing will be halted where
    technically possible. Fees for work already performed may
    not be refundable; see Refund Policy.
  - Withdrawal does not affect lawful processing already
    completed before withdrawal.
