"KnowYourGenes" (also "KYG") is a brand operated by BFG Market

We operate the website kyg.in and the related mobile/web
application (together, the "Platform"), through which we offer
at-home sample collection and genetic testing services across
Delhi NCR.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information, sensitive
personal information and genetic data we collect, how we use it,
who we share it with, how long we keep it, and the rights you have
over it. It is published in compliance with the Digital Personal
Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"), the Information Technology
(Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal
Data or Information) Rules, 2011 ("SPDI Rules"), and the Consumer
Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020.


1. SCOPE
--------
This policy applies to everyone who:
  (a) visits kyg.in or any KYG sub-domain,
  (b) creates a KYG account,
  (c) books a test or has a sample collected by a KYG phlebotomist,
  (d) receives or accesses a KYG report, or
  (e) interacts with KYG support, marketing or counselling channels.

By using the Platform or booking a service, you confirm that you
have read and understood this policy. If you are booking on behalf
of another individual, you confirm you are authorised to share their
information with us and to receive communications about them.


2. WHAT WE COLLECT
------------------
We collect only what is necessary for the services you have asked
for, fraud prevention, statutory compliance, and improving the
Platform. Specifically:

A. Identifiers and contact details
   - Full name, date of birth, sex assigned at birth, gender,
     email, mobile number, postal address with pincode.

B. Account and transaction data
   - Login credentials (passwords are hashed; we never see them
     in plaintext), order history, cart contents, coupon use,
     billing details, payment status. Card and bank details are
     handled by Razorpay and never stored on KYG servers.

C. Health and lifestyle questionnaire data ("Health Information")
   - Self-reported medical history, family history, current
     medications, allergies, dietary habits, lifestyle factors,
     and any context you choose to share with a counsellor.

D. Genetic data ("Genetic Data")
   - The biological sample (saliva / blood) collected by our
     phlebotomist, the DNA sequence variants generated from it,
     and the downstream interpretive output. Genetic Data is
     "sensitive personal information" under the SPDI Rules and is
     treated as such.

E. Device and usage data
   - IP address, browser, device, operating system, referrer,
     pages visited, session timing, and crash logs. Collected via
     first-party analytics (and Google Analytics 4 if you have
     not opted out) for product analytics and security.

F. Location data
   - The pincode and full address required to dispatch a
     phlebotomist. We do not track real-time location of users.
     Our phlebotomist's location is tracked only during an active
     collection appointment, for safety and dispatch.

G. Communications
   - Records of your interactions with our care team, WhatsApp
     messages, emails, and consultation notes from genetic
     counsellors.


3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION
------------------------------
We use the information collected to:
  - Fulfil the test you have booked — schedule the phlebotomist,
    process the sample at our partner NABL-accredited lab, and
    deliver your report.
  - Provide pre-test and post-test genetic counselling, where
    chosen.
  - Process payments, issue invoices, and process refunds.
  - Send transactional updates (booking confirmation, agent
    en-route, sample collected, report ready) over WhatsApp,
    SMS, email, or push notification.
  - Send marketing communications, only where you have given
    explicit, separate consent, and only between 09:00 and 21:00
    IST. You can withdraw consent at any time from your account
    preferences or by replying STOP to a message.
  - Investigate suspected misuse, fraud, or violation of our
    Terms of Service.
  - Comply with legal obligations and regulatory requests.
  - Improve the Platform, in aggregated and de-identified form.

We do NOT use your Genetic Data, Health Information, or contact
details for any of the following:
  - To set or influence the price of any product or service
    offered to you;
  - To market insurance, lending, or financial products to you;
  - To train any third-party AI/ML model;
  - To sell to any data broker or marketing list, anonymised or
    otherwise.


4. LEGAL BASIS (DPDP ACT)
-------------------------
We process your personal data under one of the following bases:
  - your consent, recorded explicitly at the point of collection;
  - the performance of the contract you have entered into by
    booking a test;
  - compliance with a legal obligation we are subject to;
  - protection of your vital interests (in the rare case of a
    clinically significant finding requiring follow-up).

Wherever consent is the basis, you may withdraw it through your
account or by writing to grievance@kyg.in. Withdrawal does not
affect processing already lawfully carried out.


5. HOW WE HANDLE SAMPLES AND GENETIC DATA
-----------------------------------------
A. Sample handling
   Once collected, your sample is labelled with a unique de-
   identified accession number. Your name and address are
   stripped from the physical sample before it reaches the lab.
   Only the KYG operations team and the lab's reporting
   pathologist can re-link the accession number to your identity.

B. Storage
   Samples are stored at our partner NABL-accredited lab for a
   maximum of 60 days from receipt, after which the residual
   sample is destroyed under the lab's biomedical waste protocol,
   unless you have separately consented to a longer hold for
   re-analysis.

C. Genetic Data storage
   Raw sequencing data and the variant call file ("VCF") are
   stored on KYG-controlled servers hosted in India, encrypted at
   rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+). Access is restricted
   to authorised KYG personnel on a need-to-know basis and is
   audit-logged.

D. Retention
   By default, your Genetic Data and report are retained for as
   long as you maintain an account with us, so you can re-access
   your report at any time. You may at any time:
     - download your report and have your Genetic Data deleted;
     - close your account, on which we delete your Genetic Data
       and Health Information within 60 days and retain only the
       statutory minimum (invoice records, audit logs) required
       by Indian tax and consumer protection laws.

E. No research use without separate consent
   We will not use your Genetic Data for research, publication,
   or aggregated dataset products unless you give a separate,
   explicit, opt-in consent through the Research Participation
   form. Research consent is independent of using our service and
   can be withdrawn at any time.


6. SHARING
----------
We share your information only with:
  - The NABL-accredited partner lab that processes your sample
    (de-identified by accession number; the lab does not get your
    name/address).
  - The genetic counsellor or clinician you have chosen to
    consult, if any.
  - Razorpay, our payment gateway, for processing payments.
  - Shipping / logistics partners only to the extent of name,
    phone and delivery address required to dispatch a kit, return
    a physical report, or pick up materials. The vast majority of
    our flow uses an in-house phlebotomist; no logistics partner
    sees Genetic Data.
  - Communication providers (WhatsApp Business API provider,
    SMS gateway, email provider) for transactional and consented
    marketing messages.
  - Hosting and infrastructure providers (cloud and CDN) under
    standard data-processing agreements.
  - Law enforcement, regulators, and courts, only where we are
    legally compelled, and only to the minimum extent required.

In the event KYG is acquired by, merged with, or sells assets to
another entity, your information may be transferred as part of that
transaction. We will notify you in advance and you will retain the
right to delete your data before the transfer takes effect.

We do NOT sell your information. We do not share your Genetic Data
or Health Information with insurers, employers, lenders, or
advertisers.


7. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
--------------------------
Your data is stored and processed in India. If we ever need to
transfer it outside India (for example, to a cloud region outside
India), we will do so only to countries not restricted by the
Central Government under section 16 of the DPDP Act, and under
appropriate contractual safeguards.


8. SECURITY
-----------
We follow the "reasonable security practices" standard prescribed
by the SPDI Rules. Concretely:
  - TLS 1.2+ on all public endpoints;
  - AES-256 at-rest encryption for Genetic Data and report files;
  - Role-based access control with audit logging for staff;
  - Two-factor authentication on internal admin systems;
  - Annual third-party security review;
  - Documented breach-response plan with notification to affected
    users and the Data Protection Board within the statutory
    window.

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a personal
data breach affecting you, we will notify you and the Data
Protection Board as required by the DPDP Act.


9. YOUR RIGHTS
--------------
You have the right to:
  - Access the personal data we hold about you;
  - Correct inaccurate or outdated information;
  - Have your Genetic Data and Health Information deleted (we
    retain only what statute requires);
  - Withdraw any consent you have given, at any time;
  - Nominate another person to exercise these rights in the
    event of your death or incapacity;
  - File a grievance with our Grievance Officer (details below);
  - Escalate to the Data Protection Board of India if your
    grievance is not resolved.

To exercise any of these rights, write to privacy@kyg.in. We will
respond within 30 days.
